The Director analyzes Branch programs and their promotion and achievement of strategic and business priorities; undertakes and evaluates research, environmental scans and special/pilot projects; analyzes and identifies emerging trends and uses the results to help develop strategic frameworks, products and tools, as well as to forecast future directions; develops policy, program and service delivery options with a view to optimizing innovation and effectiveness; provides direction to program managers regarding the implementation of performance measurement indicators and tools; and directs performance measurement producing knowledge products to guide policy and program interpretation and application to enhance current program and service delivery frameworks and support changes to new modern and improved ways of doing business. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE The Director, Business Analysis is one of four positions reporting to the Director General (DG), Strategic Directions. The other three are: the Director, Service Delivery/Programs Policy and Program Development, the Director, Service Delivery/Programs Partnerships and the Director, Governance and Accountability. Reporting to the Director, Business Analysis are two generic managers for which titles may be different for each of the branches. NATURE AND SCOPE Human Resources and Skills Development Canada (HRSDC s) mission is to build a stronger and more competitive Canada, to support Canadians in making choices that help them live productive and rewarding lives, and to improve Canadians quality of life. SPECIFIC ACCOUNTABILITIES Directs the conduct of short and long term research to provide the data and knowledge required to enact the transformation of business lines and enhance the Branch 's strategic and innovative orientations for the development and continuous renewal of its policy, program and service frameworks and service offerings.
