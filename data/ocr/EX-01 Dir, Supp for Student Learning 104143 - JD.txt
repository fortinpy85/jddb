The others are the Director, Stakeholder Engagement, Governance and Digital Outreach, CSC; and the Director, Policy and Program Innovation, CSC. NATURE AND SCOPE NATURE ET PORTEE DES FONCTIONS Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) is a large and complex federal government department with the greatest impact on the daily lives of Canadian citizens. ESDC is overseen by four federal Ministers and operates under the direction the Deputy Minister of Employment and Social Development, the Senior Associate Deputy Minister of Employment and Social Development and Chief Operating Officer for Service Canada, the Associate Deputy Minister of Employment and Social Development, the Deputy Minister of Labour, and the Chief Transformation Officer. The Department provides a wide range of programs and services that support Canadians at all stages of life such as seniors, unemployed workers, parents raising young children, and students. The Department fosters inclusive growth by providing opportunity and assistance to Canadians with distinct needs, such as Indigenous peoples, people with disabilities, homeless people and recent immigrants. ESDC oversees labour relations, occupational health and safety, labour standards, employment equity and workers compensation in the federal jurisdiction and delivers programs and services on behalf of other departments and agencies, such as Passport services for Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC), and to veterans on behalf of Veterans Affairs Canada. ESDC also designs and delivers a number of Government of Canada programs that provide benefits for Canadians, including Employment Insurance, Canada Pension Plan, Old Age Security, the Guaranteed Income Supplement, Canada Student Loans and Grants, Canada Apprentice Loans Program, Canada Education Savings Program; the Wage Earner Protection Program; and more recently, the Canada Emergency Response Benefits provided to assist individuals and employers experiencing income loss/financial hardship due to the 2O2O global COVID-I9 pandemic. Service Canada delivers many of ESDC s programs and services through its integrated national service delivery infrastructure (approximately 6OO delivery points) which serves as the primary point of contact for persons seeking information or to access Government of Canada services and benefits. Services are provided online via the Internet, through Service Canada s Specialized Call Centres across Canada. Service Canada continues to build its capacity to meet the ever-increasing demand for services, in particular through strategic partnerships with other levels of government and community service providers to achieve cost economies and better serve local communities. It is within this context, the Director, Supports for Student Learning Program (SSLP) is accountable for leading the Supports for Student Learning Program, which has the objective to fund a broad range of dynamic organizations operating at the national, regional, local and grassroots levels providing learners Page 2 of
with the financial and non-financial supports needed to graduate high school, transition to and persist in post-secondary education. The Director provides leadership for the development of key policy and supporting documents for the program, and forward thinking on the SSLP s policy agenda. The Director leads policy and program development to create equity of opportunity so that all students can access supports to improve their educational attainment, and acquire the skills that will help them successfully transition to the labour market. The Director provides leadership and guidance to ensure the conduct of policy analysis and research. The Director must also engage and collaborate with partners and stakeholders, as well as develops strategies in collaboration with expert organizations to test innovative solutions and to evaluate the direct impact of initiatives. For SSLP, that means working with stakeholders to provide youth with early interventions and after-school supports to help them succeed in their studies and transition to, and succeed in, PSE. The Director is responsible for leading the Implementation and Results function for the program by managing and supporting the program's key relationships with student-serving organizations, whether they be long standing or newly initiated through Budget commitments. In particular, the Director is responsible to provide leadership for supporting the development of investment strategies and Calls for Proposals. The Director plays a key role as a program policy lead alongside the Program Operations Branch on the implementation of Calls for Proposals and agreement negotiations to ensure the funded projects are aligned with the program. This involves establishing key relationships with existing funding recipients and leading the implementation of the impact measurement strategy including departmental evaluations and contributing to other corporate reporting (e.g., PIP, Departmental Plan, Departmental Results Report). As part of its core responsibilities, the Director is required to establish and maintain strong consultative, collaborative networks and partnerships with senior managers throughout the department and with other government departments, partners, and stakeholders in order to promote and support after-school programming, that help youth at risk of disengaging from their studies to graduate from, and transition to, PSE and the labour market. The Director builds and maintains key relationships with stakeholders, third- party delivery organizations and Government of Canada partners and stakeholders, and develops a presence for the SSLP in international fora and with international partners. The Director is guided by the general framework of governmental policies, regulations and directives governing the financial, security, human resources and administrative aspects of the public service. The Director enjoys considerable freedom in establishing the Division s goals and objectives, setting priorities, allocating resources, and evaluating operational effectiveness of activities in supporting ESDC objectives. The Director must apply sound management principles to ensure optimum use of Division financial and human resources approved. The Director must also foster a workplace environment where staff are encouraged to broaden their knowledge and acquire new skills necessary to perform work. The position is also accountable for establishing work priorities, supervising staff, evaluating work performance and ensuring the efficient management and use of all resources assigned to the Division.
