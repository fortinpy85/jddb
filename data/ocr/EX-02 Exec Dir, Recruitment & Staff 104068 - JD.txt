NATURE AND SCOPE NATURE ET PORTEE DES FONCTIONS ESDC, which includes Service Canada and the Canada Labour program, is one of the largest and most complex federal departments with the greatest impact on the daily lives of citizens. Overseen by four federal Ministers, the Department operates under the direction of the Deputy Minister, ESDC; the Deputy Minister of Labour and Associate Deputy Minister, ESDC; the Senior Associate Deputy Minister ESDC who is also the Chief Operating Officer of Service Canada; the Senior Associate Deputy Minister, ESDC; the Associate Deputy Minister, ESDC; and the Chief Transformation Officer. ESDC designs and delivers a number of Government of Canada programs that provide benefits for Canadians, including Employment Insurance, Canada Pension Plan, Old Age Security, the Guaranteed Income Supplement, Canada Student Loans and Grants, Canada Apprentice Loans Program, Canada Education Savings Program; the Wage Earner Protection Program; and more recently special benefits programs such as, the Canada Emergency Relief Benefits provided to assist individuals and employers experiencing income loss/financial hardship due to the 2O2O global COVID-I9 pandemic. It also delivers programs and services on behalf of other departments and agencies, such as Passport services for Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada IRCC), and for veterans on behalf of Veterans Affairs Canada. Many of ESDC s programs and services are delivered through its integrated network infrastructure which serves as a window for persons seeking information or access to Government of Canada services and benefits. Services are provided online via the Internet, through Service Canada s Specialized Call Centres, and at more than 6OO points of service across Canada. This includes some 32O+/- in-person centres, 239+/- scheduled out-reach locations, and 32+/- Government of Canada passport offices. ESDC is the third-largest department within the Government of Canada, with a workforce of 35,OOO+ located in 3OO locations across Canada. The size and diversity of the Department's business lines result in a management challenge of almost unrivaled complexity. The Human Resources Services Branch mandate involves the development and delivery of a strategic and innovative approach to people management and renewal, enabled by integrated planning, and enabling policies, programs and services to position the department to deliver on its mandates now and in the future. The Human Resources Services Branch mandate involves the development and delivery of a strategic approach to HR management and renewal, enabled by integrated planning, policies, programs and services. The Workforce Management Directorate provides authoritative advice and recommendations to the ADM of HR, the DM and client ADMs relating to innovative HR strategies, policies, structures and approaches In support of HR service delivery, and modernization initiatives. It is within this context that the Executive Director, Enterprise Recruitment, and Staffing Consultancy is accountable for the development, planning and delivery of a comprehensive array of operational recruitment and staffing services, strategies. approaches, processes, initiatives, guidelines, directives and frameworks to build, strengthen and sustain the ESDC workforce and support business transformation objectives. The Director must ensure that strategies in these areas of responsibility address both departmental and Government-wide workforce management objectives and priorities. The incumbent works in close partnership with other internal HR functions and enabling functions such as security to ensure anintegrated approach in planning, development and implementation of new recruitment and staffing initiatives. The Director acts a catalyst for cultural change in keeping with government initiatives such as public service modernization. The Director provides strategic support to management to encourage innovative approaches to achieve efficient, effective and economical human resource solutions and innovation. He/she identifies and defines recruitment and staffing challenges and formulates recommendations and strategies to resolve them. The Director leads various optimization initiatives in the branch and oversees the realignment of the advisory function for HR professionals, and centralization of key support functions (priority clearance, pool management, etc.) to optimize service delivery. The Director is responsible to ensuring the horizontal integration, efficiency and service standardization for HR approaches, services, initiatives and programs required to support departmental business and transformation priorities and plans. The Director identifies and implements collective process and outreach activities in close partnership with senior managers and manages the development of innovative assessment tools and mechanisms to support regions while at the same time providing critical advisory assistance to managers with respect to recruitment, official languages, employment equity and workforce adjustment. In order to sustain the credibility which is essential to the success of this role, the Director must maintain a thorough understanding and appreciation of the pressures and demands faced by the clients and partners in their operating environments, particularly in relation to their HR strategic, recruitment and staffing challenges and integrated human resource planning requirements, and identifies, addresses and influences community recruitment and retention issues and trends in emerging technologies. The incumbent must also maintain an awareness of new trends and developments in service delivery in order to develop and implement innovative HR service delivery models to meet client needs. As well, the Director must be sensitive to the pressures on HRSB itself in terms of delivering on its overall mandate, in an operating environment where major PS renewal initiatives and changes are being imposed. Therefore, through direct and continuous consultation and liaison with the clients and partners, the Director is accountable for strong leadership in assessing the operating environments in the context of how both HR business-specific activities and HRSB's strategic agenda can be supported by new or changed HR delivery models and initiatives. The Director works closely and collaboratively with the other Directorates, with enabling functions and the Public Service Commission (PSC) in establishing the optimal methodologies that provide the clients and partners with the services they require and fit the departmental context and business objectives. The incumbent acts as a broker, identifying potential for change: works closely with the clients and conducts consultations with hiring managers; provides a focal point and ensures that the clients and partners are kept informed of the timing and the business implications; brings an operational perspective to negotiating and mediating the rollout of new initiatives. service delivery models or strategies, and develops plans that are least disruptive to the client and which can be effectively supported through change management internal to the HR community. The Director must use highly developed interpersonal skills in order to facilitate consensus on strategies and service delivery models, and also sets out the responsibilities of the clients and partners. It is of the utmost importance to ensure that the accountabilities are well-defined, and that the expectations for each party are accepted from the outset. The incumbent provides leadership in addressing issues and events from the previous year and must be diplomatic in managing the expectations of clients and partners and strengthening the collegial relationship between HRSB, unions, the clients and partners. As well, the Director must establish and maintain flexible and efficient processes where, because of unforeseen events or sudden changes in the operating environment, particular standards must be changed, in consultation with the clients and partners, without compromising the integrity of the overall approach.
