Due to its profile. the Labour Program has been assigned its own Minister and Deputy Minister. The combined ESDC, Service Canada and Labour Program workforce exceeds 26.OOO employees located in all regions of the country, through which the programs and services of the Department, as well as those of other departments and agencies. are made available to Canadians in person, by telephone, by mail or through the Internet. One of ESDC 's business lines is the Labour Program (LP), which was created over a century ago, and which is responsible for protecting the rights and well-being of both workers and employers in federally regulated workplaces. The jurisdiction of the Labour Program includes such vital industries as rail and air transportation, banking. inter-provincial trucking, Great Lakes and international shipping, long-shoring, grain handling and all electronic modes of interprovincial and international communications. Some 955,OOO employees and I8.63O employers in the federally regulated private sector fall under this jurisdiction. The private sector includes major employers such as Air Canada. CN Rail. Bell Canada. CBC. and Canada Post Corporation, and significant labour organizations such as the Canadian Labour Congress, Unifor, Canadian Union of Postal Workers and the Teamsters. Proper delivery of programs affecting the federal jurisdiction is important in order not to disrupt or impair the ongoing activity of the organization, the sector or the economy. This jurisdiction encompasses I.2 million workers and approximately 9O% of Canada s GDP, reflecting the critical importance of industry sectors such as banking, transportation. telecommunication, oil and gas, federal Crown corporations. The goals of the Labour Program are to promote a fair, safe, healthy, stable, productive and cooperative work environment for all Canadians. This goal is achieved through the formulation, development. implementation and enforcement of legislation relating to labour law, occupational health and safety. fire prevention, labour standards. workplace equity, workers compensation, training and workplace information. The national mandate of the Branch and the diversity of industries it covers mean that decisions taken have a major impact on the formulation and implementation of Canada s workplace policies in the private sectors. In addition. the Labour Program supports effective co-operative union-management relations through the mediation, conciliation and grant programs. Finally, the Program has strong international and intergovernmental functions and represents Canada in multilateral, bilateral and national fora dealing with labour standards. co-operation and technical assistance. The Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service provides expert assistance to help resolve workplace differences. It also provides tools for workers in the federal jurisdiction to challenge unjust dismissals and to recover unpaid wages. These services and tools support cooperation and encourage fairness amongst employers and workers, leading to more productive workplaces and preventing strikes that disrupt the lives of Canadians and the economy Within this context the Director General, Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service is responsible to provide executive leadership and direction to ensure harmonious labour-management relations, which are beneficial to both the economic security of individuals and the economic prosperity of Canada. The DG leads the provision of research. collection, analysis and dissemination of information and data on collective bargaining settlements, work stoppages and labour organizations in Canada.
